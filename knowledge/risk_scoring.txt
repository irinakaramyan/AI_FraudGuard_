TITLE: Risk Scoring Methodology and Interpretation Guide
CATEGORY: risk_scoring

## Risk Score Interpretation
The combined risk score ranges from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents the probability that a transaction is fraudulent.

LOW RISK (0.00 - 0.29):
Transaction shows no significant fraud indicators. Normal customer behavior. Process without intervention.
Action: Approve automatically. No analyst review required.

MEDIUM RISK (0.30 - 0.49):
Transaction shows some minor anomalies but insufficient evidence for flagging. May indicate emerging patterns.
Action: Approve with enhanced monitoring. Log for pattern analysis. Review if customer shows repeated medium-risk scores.

HIGH RISK (0.50 - 0.74):
Transaction has multiple fraud indicators or one significant indicator. Requires investigation.
Action: FLAG transaction. Hold for analyst review. Send customer verification (OTP, email confirmation). Delay settlement.

CRITICAL RISK (0.75 - 1.00):
Transaction shows strong fraud indicators. High probability of fraudulent activity.
Action: BLOCK transaction immediately. Generate CRITICAL alert. Freeze account pending investigation. Notify customer through secure channel.

## Score Component Analysis

Rule Score Components:
When the rule score is high, examine which rules triggered:
- LARGE_AMOUNT alone is common for legitimate high-value purchases — look for corroborating signals
- HIGH_FREQUENCY suggests card testing or account takeover — take immediate action
- INTERNATIONAL_TRANSACTION needs geographic context — check if customer recently traveled
- ROUND_AMOUNT combined with other signals is highly suspicious

ML Score Analysis:
When ML score is high but rule score is low:
- The model detected statistical anomalies not captured by explicit rules
- Review the feature values: which features drove the anomaly?
- Common causes: unusual merchant, time pattern, amount relative to history
- May indicate new fraud pattern not yet captured in rules

Discordant Scores (high rule, low ML or vice versa):
- High rule, low ML: Transaction matches rule patterns but model considers it normal — may be false positive
- Low rule, high ML: Model detects statistical anomaly without explicit rule triggers — investigate carefully

## False Positive Management
False positives occur when legitimate transactions are incorrectly flagged. Best practices:
1. Establish baseline rates: Expected false positive rate for well-tuned system is 0.5-2% of transactions
2. Customer friction: Flagged transactions should trigger step-up authentication, not automatic decline
3. Feedback loop: Resolved alerts should be fed back to improve model training
4. Rule tuning: If a rule consistently generates false positives, increase its threshold or reduce weight

## Alert Prioritization Framework
Analysts should review alerts in this priority order:
1. CRITICAL severity + high dollar amount (>$5,000)
2. CRITICAL severity + international transaction
3. HIGH severity + HIGH_FREQUENCY rule triggered
4. HIGH severity + new customer (account <30 days)
5. HIGH severity + standard patterns
6. MEDIUM severity alerts (batch review)

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for Fraud Detection
- Fraud Detection Rate (FDR): Percentage of actual fraud caught by system. Target: >85%
- False Positive Rate (FPR): Percentage of legitimate transactions incorrectly flagged. Target: <2%
- Average Review Time: Time from alert generation to analyst decision. Target: <2 hours
- Chargeback Rate: Percentage of transactions resulting in chargebacks. Target: <0.5%
- System Response Time: Time from transaction submission to risk score. Target: <200ms

## Model Performance Benchmarks
Isolation Forest (unsupervised):
- Precision: ~72% (72 of 100 flagged are actually fraud)
- Recall: ~68% (catches 68% of all fraudulent transactions)
- F1 Score: ~0.70

Random Forest (supervised):
- Precision: ~88%
- Recall: ~82%
- F1 Score: ~0.85

Combined Ensemble:
- Precision: ~85%
- Recall: ~80%
- F1 Score: ~0.82
- AUC-ROC: ~0.91

## Transaction Amount Risk Profile
$0 - $100: Low inherent risk regardless of other factors
$100 - $1,000: Moderate risk, rely primarily on behavioral signals
$1,000 - $10,000: High inherent risk, apply full rule engine
$10,000+: Very high inherent risk, mandatory review for first occurrence
$50,000+: Critical — manual approval required regardless of risk score
