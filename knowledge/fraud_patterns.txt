TITLE: Common Fraud Patterns and Attack Vectors
CATEGORY: fraud_patterns

## Velocity Attacks
Velocity attacks occur when fraudsters make many transactions in a short timeframe, hoping to maximize theft before detection.
Key indicators:
- More than 5 transactions within 60 minutes from the same account
- Multiple small transactions followed by one large transaction (structuring)
- Rapid succession of declined transactions before an approved one
- Multiple card-not-present transactions across different merchants in minutes

## Card Testing / BIN Attacks
Criminals test stolen card numbers with small transactions to verify validity before making large purchases.
Indicators:
- Transactions under $1.00 at gas stations or online merchants
- Multiple failed authorization attempts before success
- Same IP address or device testing multiple card numbers
- Transactions at merchants known for easy authorization (parking, charity)

## Account Takeover (ATO)
Fraudsters gain access to legitimate customer accounts through credential theft, phishing, or social engineering.
Signs:
- Login from new device or IP address followed immediately by high-value transaction
- Password change followed by quick transaction
- Multiple failed login attempts before success
- Transaction in different geographic location than recent history

## Synthetic Identity Fraud
Criminals create fake identities by combining real and fictitious personal information.
Characteristics:
- New account with no transaction history suddenly making large purchases
- Address associated with multiple different identities
- Social Security number belongs to deceased person or minor
- Thin credit file with sudden high-value activity

## Card-Not-Present (CNP) Fraud
Fraud using stolen card details for online purchases where physical card is not required.
Patterns:
- Shipping address different from billing address
- Multiple failed CVV attempts
- High-value digital goods (gift cards, electronics, software)
- International shipping for domestic cardholders
- Order placed with express/overnight shipping

## Triangulation Fraud
Fraudster creates fake online store, collects real payment data from victims, uses stolen cards to fulfill orders.
Indicators:
- Multiple orders from different customers shipped to same address
- Merchant category mismatch with typical customer behavior
- Orders with mismatched billing and shipping information

## Refund / Return Fraud
Manipulation of refund processes to extract money from merchants or financial institutions.
Signs:
- Frequent refund requests on high-value items
- Refunds requested immediately after purchase
- Multiple refunds across similar transaction amounts
- Return of different item than purchased

## Money Mule Transactions
Fraudsters use intermediary accounts to move stolen funds, making tracing difficult.
Patterns:
- Account receiving funds from multiple unknown sources then immediately transferring out
- Round-dollar transfers (exactly $500, $1000, $5000)
- Multiple small incoming transfers followed by one large outgoing transfer
- Wire transfers to high-risk jurisdictions (NG, RU, UA, IR, KP)

## Geographical Anomalies
Transactions occurring in locations inconsistent with the customer's established patterns.
Indicators:
- Transaction in foreign country without prior travel notifications
- Two transactions in physically impossible locations within short timeframe (velocity of location)
- Transaction at unusual hour for the customer's timezone
- IP address geolocation does not match transaction location

## First-Party Fraud (Friendly Fraud)
Legitimate customers who dispute legitimate charges or abuse merchant policies.
Signs:
- Customer disputes transaction they made themselves
- Pattern of chargebacks after high-value purchases
- Account history shows legitimate use followed by sudden dispute activity
